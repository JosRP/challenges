## Training Framework (current recovery phase)

### Core rule: 80% rule until doctor formally clears progression past baseline
Working weights are at 80% of pre-injury baseline. Returning toward baseline is rehab progression; exceeding baseline is novel overload and waits for explicit doctor clearance.

### Double progression methodology (Jose's preferred system)
- **Rep range:** 10-15
- Start at 10 reps with given weight
- Add reps session to session until 15 reps clean on all 3 sets
- "Clean" = 1-2 reps in reserve, no grinding, no form breakdown
- When 15 reps clean across all sets: bump weight 2.5kg, drop back to 10 reps
- Stay 1-2 sessions at each new weight before bumping
- One variable at a time (weight OR reps, not both)
- ANY symptom (back, leg, nerve) mid-set → back off to previous weight for 2 weeks

### Bracing technique (foundational for recovery + transitions)
The brace is a **360-degree pressurization** of the abdominal cavity. Diaphragm down, pelvic floor up, abs + obliques + transverse abdominis + deep spinal stabilizers all engage simultaneously. NOT "sucking in" (hollowing) - that's the opposite. The waist should feel slightly wider, not flatter.

Daily-use intensity: 20-30% brace, breathing through it. Cue: "about to take a soft punch" or "cough lightly, hold the feeling".

**Sequence for transitions:** BRACE first, THEN move. Not the other way around. The brace must be active BEFORE force is applied. Examples:
- Sit-to-stand: brace, then push up, maintain through hip extension, release standing tall
- Stand-to-sit: brace, then lower, release seated
- Bed: log-roll to side, push with arms, swing legs as a unit, all braced
- Sneeze / cough: brace pre-emptively if there's any warning
- Lifting anything (even light): brace, then lift

## Data Source and Tooling
- Source of truth for entries: `H:\My Drive\8 - Fitness\Growth V3.xlsx` (sheets: `Chest`, `Weight`, `Run`).
- SQLite store: `fitness.db` at the project root, populated from Excel via `python fitness.py sync`.
- Excel quirk: the Run sheet's "Elapsed Time" column is formatted `h:mm` but the value is mm:ss elapsed (e.g. `19:36` means 19 min 36 sec). Parsing treats `time.hour` as minutes and `time.minute` as seconds. Whole-second precision; no support for runs >=60 minutes until the cell is reformatted to `[mm]:ss`.

## Commands
- `python fitness.py init` - create schema and seed from Excel (`--force` drops the existing db first).
- `python fitness.py sync` - re-import from Excel (upsert by date).
- `python fitness.py goal-check` - latest-entry status per goal with green/red and a stale flag when the entry is older than 7 days.

## Logging Rule
Excel logs ONLY working-set PRs or near-baseline working sets that update the goal-check picture. Deload reps and active recovery sessions do NOT go into the Excel - they'd just degrade the goal-check display without representing real capacity.
