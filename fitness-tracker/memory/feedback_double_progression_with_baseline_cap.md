Jose's preferred progression methodology is **double progression** within a 10-15 rep range: start at 10 reps with a given weight, add reps session to session until 15 clean reps on all 3 sets, then bump weight 2.5kg and drop back to 10. Standardized 3 sets across all exercises for cognitive simplicity (only weight and reps vary).

**Why:** Jose validated this as his preferred system on 2026-05-28 (he proposed it himself, refining the simpler "hold reps in a band" approach). Double progression auto-regulates by demanding earned rep capacity before weight increases, which is mechanically safer than pure weight progression. Single-variable-change-per-session reduces ambiguity in reading body feedback.

**How to apply:**
- Rep range 10-15. "Clean" reps = 1-2 in reserve, no grinding, no form breakdown.
- Bump weight only when ALL 3 sets hit 15 clean reps. Not the lead set, not "one good set". All three.
- 2.5kg increments. Stay 1-2 sessions at each weight before bumping.
- During recovery, the bump ceiling is **pre-injury baseline weight** (chest 75kg, lat pulldown 45kg, face pulls 17.5kg, tricep pushdown 20kg, wrist curls 28.75kg). The bump trigger (15 clean reps x 3 sets) is SUSPENDED at cap weight. Hitting 15x3 at cap is the steady state during recovery, NOT a trigger to bump to 20kg / 47.5kg / etc. Bank the clean session and stay at the cap. The 2.5kg bump unlocks only after a doctor formally clears progression at the next consult. Do not self-authorize a bump based on feeling good, SLR improvement, or "earned" reps — caps are the constraint, not double progression.
- ANY symptom (back, leg, nerve) mid-set: back off to previous weight for 2 weeks.
- The principle that protects the lifts that matter (chest press, lat pulldown) is enforced on the lifts that don't (face pulls). Discipline on the low-stakes lifts prevents creep on the high-stakes ones. Don't let rep creep past the cap on any lift "because it's low risk".
