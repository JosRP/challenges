The studies below are the landmark evidence base for the recurring claims in the L5-S1 recovery project. When discussing imaging findings, herniation reabsorption odds, asymptomatic populations, or spinal stability concepts, cite these by author + journal + year rather than paraphrasing from memory. Citations are from memory and should be verified on PubMed before being used in any writing Jose shares externally.

**Imaging-symptom decoupling (the headline claim that imaging findings do not predict symptoms):**

- Brinjikji W et al., AJNR 2015;36(4):811-816. Systematic review of imaging features of spinal degeneration in asymptomatic populations. Compiled 33 articles, 3,110 asymptomatic individuals. Disc degeneration in 37% of 20-year-olds, 80% of 50-year-olds, 96% of 80-year-olds. Disc bulges in 30% of 20-year-olds, 84% of 80-year-olds. The modern definitive citation for the imaging-symptom decoupling.
- Boden SD et al., JBJS 1990;72(3):403-408. Original asymptomatic MRI study.
- Jensen MC et al., NEJM 1994;331(2):69-73. MRI of the lumbar spine in people without back pain.
- Borenstein DG et al., JBJS 2001;83-A(9):1306-1311. 7-year longitudinal follow-up of MRI findings vs symptoms.

**Disc grading (T2 signal-based classification):**

- Pfirrmann CW et al., Spine 2001;26(17):1873-1878. Magnetic resonance classification of lumbar intervertebral disc degeneration. Grades I-V based on T2 signal and structure. This is the standard radiology classification taught in every musculoskeletal radiology course.

**Herniation natural history / spontaneous regression rates:**

- Chiu CC et al., Clinical Rehabilitation 2015;29(2):184-195. The probability of spontaneous regression of lumbar herniated disc: a systematic review. Meta-analysis pooling multiple studies. Regression rates:
  - Sequestrated: ~96%
  - Extruded: ~70%
  - Protruded: ~41%
  - Bulging: ~13%
- This is the citation supporting the "smaller contained herniations regress more slowly" claim. Slower != does not happen; symptom resolution does not require full regression.

**Cauda equina red flags / low back pain triage:**

- NICE Guideline NG59, Low back pain and sciatica in over 16s. UK national guideline, freely accessible.
- North American Spine Society guidelines.
- ACR Appropriateness Criteria.

**Spinal stability (the 3-subsystem model):**

- Panjabi MM, J Spinal Disord 1992;5(4):383-389. The stabilizing system of the spine. Part I. Function, dysfunction, adaptation, and enhancement. Plus Part II in the same journal. Foundational paper; introduces the passive / active / neural three-subsystem framework cited thousands of times.

**Deep core / lumbopelvic stabilization training:**

- Hodges PW, Richardson CA, Spine 1996;21(22):2640-2650. Foundational transversus abdominis work.
- Richardson C, Hodges P, Hides J, Therapeutic Exercise for Lumbopelvic Stabilization. Textbook.
- McGill SM, Ultimate Back Fitness and Performance, and Back Mechanic. Books, applied biomechanics.
- Cholewicki J, McGill SM, Clin Biomech 1996. Spinal stability and muscle activation patterns.

**Glute-lumbar load distribution / "gluteal amnesia":**

- Janda V, Muscle Function Testing. Older classic on muscle imbalances and glute inhibition.
- Modern follow-ups in physiotherapy literature; specific citations vary by sub-topic.

**Panic attack physiology:**

- DSM-5 (American Psychiatric Association, 2013). Definition of panic attack: typically peaks within 10 minutes, full episode usually self-limits within 20-30 minutes.
- Hofmann SG et al., extensive work on cognitive-behavioral framework for panic disorder.

**NSAID pharmacology for radicular pain:**

- Aceclofenac (Airtal) Summary of Product Characteristics. Public document.
- Pinto RZ et al., Br J Sports Med 2017. Review of pharmacotherapy for lumbar radiculopathy.

**Notes on use:**

- These are from memory and should be verified on PubMed before being used in any externally-shared writing. Exact issue, page numbers, or author orderings may be slightly off; the studies themselves are real and well-known.
- The 80% conservative-resolution figure Dr. Almeida quoted is his case-specific read, not a universal number from these studies. Population-level resolution ranges 50-90% depending on subtype and how "resolution" is defined.
