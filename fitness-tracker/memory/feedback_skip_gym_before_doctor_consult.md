For high-stakes medical consultations (assessment, treatment planning), skip the morning gym session. Train any other day, but the consult day is rest.

**Why:** The doctor assesses you based on your CURRENT baseline. Mild post-session stiffness or a slight nerve twinge from a set during the morning workout contaminates that assessment. You don't want the conversation to be "well, you might be at level X but you also trained two hours ago, so let's discount that data point". One missed day for a high-stakes appointment is a much smaller cost than a contaminated clinical picture.

**How to apply:**
- Day-of doctor consult: no gym in the morning.
- Routine survives a single planned skip for a specific reason. This is not laziness, it's strategy.
- This does NOT apply to thermal treatment days, physio sessions, or routine check-ins - those are part of the protocol and aren't trying to read a "clean baseline".
- After the consult, training resumes normally (or per whatever the new plan dictates).
