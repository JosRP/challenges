Jose can self-administer a Straight Leg Raise (SLR) test by lying on his back and lifting the right leg, comparing range to prior sessions. This is the closest thing to an objective recovery metric he can measure himself. Improvement over time (especially in an unmedicated state) is real signal that nerve mobility is recovering and the disc inflammation is settling.

**Why:** On 2026-05-25 the doctor's SLR was very limited bilaterally (crossed SLR pattern). By 2026-05-29, with last Airtal dose ~12+ hours prior (largely cleared), Jose could raise his right leg much higher. That improvement in unmedicated range = real healing, not medication mask. Genuinely encouraging trend.

**How to apply:**
- Track informal SLR self-test as part of recovery trajectory. Note range + medication status when checking.
- BUT: SLR improvement is the thing to **bring to the next medical consult** to get progression formally cleared. It is NOT self-authorization to push past weight caps.
- If Jose says "I can lift my leg higher now, so I'll go past the cap on X" - hold the line. The clinical clearance is what unlocks the next progression phase, not the self-test. The self-test is the evidence to bring to that clearance conversation.
- This rule was tested and held on 2026-05-29 when face pulls crept to 18.75kg above the 17.5kg cap; reverted to 17.5kg per the principle.
