No couch sleeping at any point in the day during L5-S1 recovery. Specifically NEVER between waking up and going to the gym. Pool coach at Caldas surfaced this 2026-06-11 during the flare debrief: Jose's habit of falling asleep on the couch for up to ~1h between waking and going to the gym was pre-loading the spine in a guarded asymmetric position before training.

**Why:** Couch sleeping forces unsupported asymmetric posture for an extended period (head on armrest, shoulders twisted, lumbar unsupported, one leg dangling). The paraspinal muscles hold a sustained splint pattern for the duration. For an L5-S1 herniation patient mid-recovery, this is doubly bad:

1. **Standalone:** triggers contralateral or same-side paraspinal guarding that lingers for hours. Verified 2026-06-08: 2.5h couch nap left contralateral lumbar discomfort that took ~24h with movement + heat to resolve.
2. **Before training:** pre-loads the spine in a guarded state, so the subsequent gym session loads an already-compromised system. Identified by the pool coach as a contributing factor to the 2026-06-10 flare (back-to-back gym days + couch nap before each + NSAID just discontinued). The compound was significantly worse than any single factor.

**How to apply:**
- If tired before the gym: either go to bed properly with a pillow under the knees for 30 min, or push training to later. Couch nap is never the right answer.
- Anywhere in the day: no couch napping during the recovery phase. Move to the bed.
- If accidentally fallen asleep on couch: walk + brace through transitions + heat patch on the splinted side within 30 min of waking. Don't try to push immediately into normal activity.
- Post-recovery (when cleared): a 20-min intentional nap in a balanced position is fine; falling asleep for 1h+ in an asymmetric couch posture is never advisable for anyone with a herniation history.

This is distinct from [[emotional-setback-cascade-pattern]] which is the gaming / sitting / late-bed cascade. Couch sleeping is its own mechanical failure mode and should be flagged independently; it can happen even when the emotional cascade isn't active (e.g. fatigue from a 5am work topic, slow morning, accidental doze-off).
