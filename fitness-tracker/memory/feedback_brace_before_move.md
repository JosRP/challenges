The brace is a 360-degree pressurization of the abdominal cavity that creates intra-abdominal pressure (IAP), which hydraulically stabilizes the spine. For any transition or loaded action, the sequence is **brace first, then move** - not the other way around.

**Why:** Jose discovered by feel that bracing during sit-stand transitions dramatically reduced his sciatic flare. This validates that the disc IS responding to load patterns mechanically (good prognostic sign) and that IAP is the active self-management technique. If you brace AFTER initiating movement, the load has already hit the disc unbraced.

**How to apply:** For every sit-to-stand, stand-to-sit, log-roll out of bed, pre-cough/sneeze, pre-lift (even light), pre-chest-press-pedal: cue the brace at 20-30% (cough-firm, breathe through it), THEN move. Maintain through the transition, release once stable. This is the foundational self-management technique for the entire recovery period and beyond. Don't soften this rule when symptoms feel better.
