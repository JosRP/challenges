When Jose takes an emotional hit (bad doctor news, breakup, work stress), the cascade is: one bad evening of binge gaming or distraction -> prolonged sitting -> late bedtime -> missed gym next day -> potentially multiple missed days. Jose is self-aware enough to flag it openly when it happens (which is the right behavior).

**Why:** Observed three times during this recovery period. (1) After 2026-05-25 consult news (more meds, more treatment, surgery still 20% possibility), Jose binge-played a video game that evening and missed Tuesday gym. (2) After breakup on/around 2026-06-02, missed Monday and Tuesday gym, almost missed Wednesday morning. (3) After 2026-06-13 Saturday morning consult (clinical-but-cold delivery, disc desiccation noted, "small contained herniation may reabsorb slower" framing), Jose cried himself to sleep on arriving home and woke from sleep with a documented panic attack. Compound stressors (flare + recent breakup + cold consult on the same day) breached the nervous-system threshold acutely rather than via the slow-burn binge pattern.

The prolonged sitting during binges is the disc-aggravating concern. The panic attack manifestation is the nervous-system-overload manifestation. Both are responses to the same underlying load-on-the-system pattern. Different surface, same root.

**How to apply:**
- Acknowledge the emotional impact without moralizing about the missed gym. Don't shame the coping mechanism - it's human.
- Focus the practical advice on the SITTING component (the disc-relevant problem) more than the missed-gym component (recoverable in a day).
- The pattern to flag: one bad day -> multiple bad days. Going to the gym today, even after missing two, breaks the cascade. Small wins matter more than session quality.
- During emotional stress: extra sleep, regular meals even without appetite, more walking pad movement (parasympathetic regulation), extra brace discipline (emotional stress raises muscle guarding).
- If patterns extend (binge gaming, isolation, drinking) past a few days, name it explicitly so it doesn't compound silently.
- **Panic attack response (added 2026-06-13):** If Jose reports a panic attack in the context of compound stressors, validate immediately ("real, proportional to the load you are carrying, passes within 20-30 min, does not damage anything"). Don't reflexively reach for clinical reassurance about the disc as the answer; the panic is responding to a stack, not just the disc. Specific grounding action: encourage him to reach one human by voice (not text), eat something, no Reddit / no doom search, and rest in supine. The compound-stress threshold for panic appears to be triggered by 3+ stressors stacking on the same day. Watch for the stacking pattern, not just any single stressor.
