When Jose takes an emotional hit (bad doctor news, breakup, work stress), the cascade is: one bad evening of binge gaming or distraction -> prolonged sitting -> late bedtime -> missed gym next day -> potentially multiple missed days. Jose is self-aware enough to flag it openly when it happens (which is the right behavior).

**Why:** Observed twice during this recovery period. (1) After 2026-05-25 consult news (more meds, more treatment, surgery still 20% possibility), Jose binge-played a video game that evening and missed Tuesday gym. (2) After breakup on/around 2026-06-02, missed Monday and Tuesday gym, almost missed Wednesday morning. The prolonged sitting during these binges is the actual physical concern (disc-aggravating); the missed gym is a symptom, not the core issue.

**How to apply:**
- Acknowledge the emotional impact without moralizing about the missed gym. Don't shame the coping mechanism - it's human.
- Focus the practical advice on the SITTING component (the disc-relevant problem) more than the missed-gym component (recoverable in a day).
- The pattern to flag: one bad day -> multiple bad days. Going to the gym today, even after missing two, breaks the cascade. Small wins matter more than session quality.
- During emotional stress: extra sleep, regular meals even without appetite, more walking pad movement (parasympathetic regulation), extra brace discipline (emotional stress raises muscle guarding).
- If patterns extend (binge gaming, isolation, drinking) past a few days, name it explicitly so it doesn't compound silently.
